Introduction

Contact networks between individuals play a significant role in influenza transmission. However, hitherto, many strategies to control influenza transmission have been designed without a consideration for a difference in contact networks by different regions. The pattern of contact networks in each region is changed by age distributions, economic activities, social status and et al, and thus yielding a different influenza outcome by regions. To investigate these issues, the main objectives of this project are two-fold: 1) to simulate influenza outbreaks at different contact network patterns by regions, and 2) to assess a difference in the outcomes of influenza pandemics between regions.
